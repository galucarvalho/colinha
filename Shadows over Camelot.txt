    "section": "Beginning the game and Recommendations for first-time play",
    "content": "8\n; Beginning the game ;\nAt the start of the game, in a gesture of the collaboration to come, each player must select one White card\nfrom his hand and place it, face up, on the Round Table. A discussion should ensue about how to best\nshare those cards among all the Knights.\nA Knight, in a noble gesture, may voluntarily take none of the shared cards, while others may pick up\nseveral. If there is disagreement (not a good omen of things to come!), the offered cards are shuffled and\nredistributed randomly to each player.\nYou are now ready to begin.\n; Recommendations for first-time play ;\nThis game is different, really different! You will at once be playing the game, yet involuntarily helping it\ndefeat you. While the game’s mechanics are quite simple, their implications and the potential repercussions\nof your acts will be anything but.\nAs a result, unless your group of players is made up of seasoned veterans who have all thoroughly familiarized\nthemselves with these rules and appendices beforehand, we suggest you play your first game without a\npotential Traitor in your midst. It will lower the tension and fun that can stem from a good bit of back-\nstabbing, but ensure that everyone gets a chance to master\nthe game.\nTo do so, simply remove the Traitor from the mix of Loyalty\ncards before dealing those out, and use each Loyalty card\nface up, solely as a reminder of the game’s Victory\nconditions.\nThis should allow you to rapidly master the game’s\nmechanics and familiarize yourselves with the unique\nSpecial Power of each Knight, and with the Quests and\nobstacles you are likely to face.\nIf the rules seem a bit daunting at first, do not worry: All\nyou need to get started is to familiarize yourselves with\nthe game’s key components during set-up, and get a firm\ngrasp of the basic game turn structure as outlined on your\nCoat of Arms and in these rules.\nEquipped with that knowledge, you can then start the\ngame and discover the rest as you play, referring to the Book\nof Quests when entering a new Quest and to the Cards\nManifest if an occasional question arises.\nOnce you have a game under your belt, add the Traitor\ncard back into the mix of Loyalty cards at the start of the\ngame and enjoy! But remember: Someone in your valiant\ngroup might now be secretly working against the rest…\nand he is as intimately familiar with the game’s subtleties\nas you are!\n8\nk A word on k\ncollaborating\nA\nt times throughout the game, victory will hinge on\nyour group’s ability to make the right choices or\nsacrifices for the common good. The cards you are dealt,\nand your unique Special Power, may occasionally dictate\nyour choice of Quest or course of action. Collaboration\nwith other like-minded Knights in your group will often\nturn out to be crucial to winning.\nA few rules must thus be observed at all times, when\nconferring with your fellow Knights.\nDeclarations of intent (“I heard those <blasphemous> Saxons\nare on the march again, I will put them down!”)can be made\nfreely; resources (“My Men-at-arms are strong and ready,\nmy Lord”) and capabilities (“What a laughably puny Black\nKnight I see across this bridge!”)can all be discussed openly,\nas long as your comments are general and nonspecific.\nHowever, you must never reveal or discuss the explicit\nvalues of cards in your hand, or volunteer any other specific\ngame information not readily available to your fellow\nplayers. For example, you should never say “I have 3 Grails,\nlet me have this one”or “I’ll trade your three Fight 2 cards\nfor my Fight 5”.\nBeing grown men of valor, you should act of your own free\nwill and conscience. Regardless of your brethren’s advice,\nwishes or threats, you may always embark on the course\nof action of your choice. It is permissible to lie about your\nintent or your resources at hand (a particularly useful ploy\nfor a Traitor), though you must never cheat."
